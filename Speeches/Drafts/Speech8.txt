🥇 1. What is Compounding?

Let’s forget money for a second.
Think about your habits.

You skip one gym session — no big deal.
You skip two — still okay.
Skip for a month — your stomach starts looking like it’s expecting twins.

Compounding is simple:
Small efforts done consistently turn into big results.

Like brushing teeth twice a day — it compounds into a smile.
Skipping it compounds into… social distancing.

Even your mom uses compounding:
“Beta, study today.”
Next day: “Beta, you’re wasting your time.”
Next week: “Beta, even your neighbor’s son has an MBA!”
That’s emotional compounding! 😄

⸻

🥈 2. How Does Compounding Work?

Let me explain with my fitness journey.
I decided to do 10 push-ups every day.
Day 1 — I did 10.
Day 2 — 12.
Day 3 — I watched motivational videos about people doing push-ups.

After a week, I realized — compounding only works if you work.
Watching doesn’t count — unless you’re investing in Netflix.

Compounding works silently.
You don’t see results daily.
Just like your salary — it disappears silently too.

But one day — boom!
You realize your small actions have built something big.
Like your stomach — or your success. 😜

⸻

🥉 3. Why Should You Do It?

Because compounding is the only magic that actually works —
and doesn’t require a Hogwarts degree.

Every day, you’re compounding something —
Your skills, your stress, or your excuses.

If you read one page a day — that’s wisdom.
If you scroll one reel a day — that’s… carpal tunnel.

And remember — compounding doesn’t only apply to money.
It applies to kindness, discipline, and even sarcasm.
Try being sarcastic every day — you’ll either become funny… or single.
